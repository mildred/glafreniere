THE  VIRTUAL  DOPPLER  EFFECT

While the reception device is moving through the wave medium, one must consider the reception rate, not the true frequency. However all happens as if the receiver was at rest, and as if the emitter was moving.

The formulas for this are the opposite, and so, while both the emitter and the receiver are moving inside the same frame of reference, there is no observable Doppler effect.

1 – The receiver is moving towards the emitter:  F' (reception rate) = F (1 + b)

2 – The receiver is moving away from the emitter:  F' = F (1 – b)

3 – The receiver is moving according to a phi  j  angle:  F' = F (1 + b cos j)

Let us suppose that you are driving at 100 km/h while following an ambulance which speed is also 100 km/h. Its siren's is emitting a sound which frequency is worth 440 Hz. The normal Doppler effect is worth:

F = 440 Hz          b = .0817          F' = 440 / (1 + b) or 406.8 Hz.

The absolute frequency is 406.8 Hz. However you are moving toward the emitter at 100 km/h. Then the reception rate will be raised to:

F = 406.8 Hz          b = 0.0817          F' (reception rate) = 406.8 (1 + b) or 440 Hz.

Finally, inside a moving frame of reference, one obtains: F' = F (1 + b) / (1 + b)  and so:

F ' = F.

This means that whatever his speed is, one cannot detect the aether's displacement by the means of the Doppler effect.
