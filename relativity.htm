Al Kashi theorem and the relative Doppler effect.

The relative Doppler effect works according to Al Kashi's theorem. First the opposite side of the  j  angle represents one light second squared, which remains one light second. Next, the beta  b  value may represent any adjacent side. Then the Doppler effect, as seen from the moving source, will vary according to "a", which is the triangle's third side :

a 2 + b 2 + 2 . a . b . cos j  =  1

However this formula is rather difficult to work with. Any pocket calculator will do a better job with the same triangle evaluated by elementary trigonometry.

Then the relative speed  S  of light, the time  t  in seconds to cross the distance  L  in light seconds, or the Doppler effect in wavelength, all in absolute measures, can be evalueted like this :

S = cos (arc sin (b sin j) ) – b cos j

t = L / (cos (arc sin (b sin j) ) – b cos j)

l' = l (cos (arc sin (b sin j) ) – b cos j)

Note that the  j  angle is null (0°) toward the front. It reaches 180° towards the rear. One must use these formulas to evaluate the Michelson interferometer.
